Cookie Policy

Last updated: December 2025

ZAS Safeguard uses minimal local storage to make the product work. We do not use advertising cookies. We do not track you across other websites. Here is exactly what is stored and why.

What is stored and why

  • Firebase authentication token — stored in your browser's local storage when you log in. Used to authenticate you to the ZAS Safeguard backend. Expires after 1 hour and is automatically refreshed while you are logged in. Deleted when you log out.
  • Extension ID — a string identifying your installed ZAS Safeguard browser extension. Stored in localStorage so the dashboard can communicate with the extension. Not a tracking identifier.
  • User preferences — settings you configure in the dashboard (notification preferences, quiet hours, etc.) are stored in your user account on our servers, not in a cookie.
  • Stripe session — when you go through the payment flow, Stripe sets a session cookie to process your payment. This cookie is set by Stripe, not by ZAS Safeguard, and is deleted after your payment is complete. Stripe's cookie policy: stripe.com/privacy

What we do NOT store

  • No advertising or tracking cookies
  • No third-party analytics cookies (we do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or similar)
  • No cross-site tracking of any kind
  • No fingerprinting

How to clear stored data

To remove everything ZAS Safeguard has stored in your browser:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Clear browsing data → Cookies and site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Clear Data
  • Safari: Settings → Privacy → Manage Website Data

Clearing this data will log you out of the dashboard and disconnect the extension until you log in again.

Contact

If you have questions about this policy: privacy@zassafeguard.com
